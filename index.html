🏺 Step Pyramid of Djoser

The first monumental stone structure in Ancient Egypt

📜 History

The Step Pyramid of Djoser was built around 2670 BCE in Saqqara, Egypt, during the Third Dynasty. It was constructed for Pharaoh Djoser and designed by his vizier and architect Imhotep.

Before this pyramid, Egyptian rulers were buried in flat-roofed tombs called mastabas. Imhotep revolutionized architecture by stacking mastabas on top of each other, creating the first large stone structure in history.

This innovation became the foundation of all later pyramid construction in Egypt, including the famous pyramids of Giza.

🏗️ Structure

The pyramid originally began as a single mastaba but was expanded into six stepped layers. Each layer is smaller than the one below it, creating a stable, symmetrical structure.

It is built from limestone blocks and stands about 60 meters tall. It is part of a larger funerary complex with courtyards and temples.
