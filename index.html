🏺 Step Pyramid of Djoser

The world’s first monumental stone structure, built in Ancient Egypt

📜 The Story

The Step Pyramid of Djoser was built around 2670 BCE in Saqqara during Egypt’s Third Dynasty. It was created for Pharaoh Djoser and designed by his brilliant vizier and architect, Imhotep.

Before this pyramid, Egyptian kings were buried in simple flat tombs called mastabas. Imhotep revolutionized architecture by stacking mastabas on top of each other, creating the first large stone structure in history.

This innovation marked the beginning of pyramid construction in Egypt, influencing all later pyramids, including the Great Pyramids of Giza.

🏗️ Construction

The pyramid was built using limestone blocks and originally began as a single mastaba. Over time, it was expanded into six stepped layers, each smaller than the one below it.

It stands about 60 meters tall and is part of a large funerary complex including temples, courtyards, and ceremonial spaces.
