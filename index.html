🏺 STEP PYRAMID OF DJOSER

Ancient Egypt’s First Monumental Stone Structure

🔎 Quick Facts

📜 History

The Step Pyramid of Djoser was built around 2670 BCE in Saqqara during the Third Dynasty of Ancient Egypt. It was created for Pharaoh Djoser and designed by Imhotep.

It began as a simple mastaba tomb, but was expanded into a six-step pyramid, marking the birth of monumental stone architecture in human history.

🧠 Imhotep

Imhotep was a genius architect, priest, and advisor. He designed the pyramid and changed architecture forever. He was later worshipped as a god of wisdom and medicine.

🏛️ Funerary Complex

The pyramid was part of a sacred complex with courtyards, temples, and ritual spaces used for ceremonies and the king’s afterlife journey.

🧱 Underground Structure

Below the pyramid lies a 5km tunnel system with burial chambers and decorated corridors, built to protect the king forever.

🌍 Legacy

This pyramid inspired all later pyramids in Egypt, including Giza. It was the first step toward perfect pyramid engineering.
